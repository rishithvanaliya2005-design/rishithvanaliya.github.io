PINN — Radiofrequency (RF) Ablation for Breast Cancer

Building on our PINN foundations, this project applies Physics-Informed Neural Networks to model radiofrequency ablation (RFA) for breast cancer treatment by integrating PINNs with OpenFOAM simulations.

Objective

  • Model the thermal response of malignant breast tissue during radiofrequency ablation.
  • Predict tumor behavior (temperature evolution and lesion formation) under RFA exposure.
  • Optimize treatment parameters to improve efficacy while minimizing collateral damage.

Summary

This work integrates PINNs with OpenFOAM to simulate heat-induced tumor destruction in the context of RF ablation. By embedding the governing bioheat physics into a neural framework, the approach aims to predict spatiotemporal temperature fields and lesion growth without relying solely on mesh-based solvers, while enabling parameter inference from limited measurements.

Approach (high level)

The method couples physics-constrained learning with numerical simulation data to capture the thermal response of tissue during RF exposure. PINNs are used to enforce the relevant bioheat physics while leveraging OpenFOAM-generated data for guidance and validation. The ultimate goal is a model that can support faster-than-FEM predictions, inform electrode/power settings, and assist in treatment planning.

Intended outcomes

  • Accurate prediction of temperature fields and lesion formation during RFA.
  • Capability to infer tissue properties relevant to treatment response (e.g., effective conductivity, perfusion) from sparse data.
  • Tools to optimize electrode placement and power/time parameters to improve clinical outcomes.

Impact & recognition

The interdisciplinary potential of this work led to a research-based internship offer at IIT Bombay, where the project will be advanced under the mentorship of Prof. Atul Sharma.

Media & reproducibility

The project integrates PINNs with OpenFOAM-generated datasets (synthetic or experimental) to enable validation and reproducibility. Code, datasets, and reproduction instructions are organized in a project repository (link to be added).
