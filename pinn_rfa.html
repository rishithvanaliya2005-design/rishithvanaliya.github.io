PINN Radiofrequency Ablation for Breast Cancer

Biomedical Application of Physics-Informed Neural Networks

Research Overview

This work extends our PINN research into a biomedical application: modelling breast cancer treatment using Physics-Informed Neural Networks combined with OpenFOAM-based thermal simulations.

Objective

  • Model the thermal response of malignant breast tissue during radiofrequency ablation.
  • Predict tumor behavior during RFA exposure.
  • Optimize treatment parameters for more effective and minimally invasive therapy.

Summary

Building on the methodologies and insights gained from our PINN study on fluid flow benchmarks, this project applies physics-informed learning to the problem of RF ablation. By integrating PINNs with OpenFOAM simulations, the work aims to capture heat-induced tumor destruction while retaining the advantages of mesh-free learning. The approach leverages the capability of PINNs to embed governing physics directly into the model, improving interpretability and enabling accurate prediction of tissue behavior under thermal loading.

Research Significance

This project represents an interdisciplinary extension of our PINN research and demonstrates the adaptability of physics-constrained neural networks to biomedical heat-transfer problems. It highlights the potential of physics-informed learning frameworks in advancing minimally invasive cancer therapies, as well as their broader applicability to complex bioheat transfer and treatment optimization challenges.
