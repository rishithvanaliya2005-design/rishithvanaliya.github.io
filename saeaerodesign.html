SAE Aero Design West 2025 — Micro Class

Role: Team Captain — led a 30+ member student team through complete UAV design, fabrication, testing, and competition execution.

Overview

SAE Aero Design West is an international engineering competition organized by SAE International, featuring Micro, Regular, and Advanced classes. The Micro Class emphasizes maximizing payload capacity while minimizing weight and wingspan, using liquid payload to test structural efficiency and flight performance. Over 70 global university teams participated in the 2025 edition.

My contribution

As Team Captain, I managed subsystem coordination, scheduling, structural–aerodynamic integration, and mission preparation. I oversaw the full development cycle—from conceptual design and simulations to fabrication, ground testing, and flight operations under competition constraints.

Aircraft & performance

Our UAV was engineered for autonomous flight with a takeoff distance under 10 ft and a strict 450 W power limit. With an empty weight of 1.6 kg, the aircraft successfully carried a 2 L liquid payload, achieving a payload fraction above 0.5. The structural layout enabled strength, safety, and rapid assembly, while aerodynamic refinement and field-testing delivered stable performance and over 5 minutes of endurance.

Result

Through simulation-driven design, iterative prototyping, and extensive testing, the team secured 11th place overall and 5th place in mission score among more than seventy international teams.
