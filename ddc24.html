DDC Micro — 2024

Team Arrow designed a high payload-fraction micro-UAV balancing lightweight construction and structural robustness. As Design Lead, I focused on fuselage design, structural reinforcement, and verification testing.

Snapshot

  • Category: DDC Micro 2024.
  • Role: Design Lead - fuselage design, structural analysis, manufacturing guidance.
  • Result highlights: payload fraction 0.635; lift 22.9 N; thrust 15.07 N; empty weight 0.772 kg. (Report summary).
  • Tools: SolidWorks, ANSYS (CFD & FEA), XFLR5, Fusion360 topology module.

Outcome

  • Overall 2nd Place — DDC Micro 2024.
  • Best Technical Presentation.
  • Manufacturable, repairable fuselage using fliteboard + aeroply; assembly time ~30-40 s.

Key design decisions

  • Airfoil: S1223, thickened to 120% for higher lift while accepting modest Cd increase.
  • Wing: Trailing-edge tapered, span 1.0 m, area 0.17 m², aspect ratio 5.71.
  • Fuselage: Cockpit-type fliteboard with aeroply reinforcement for impact resilience and internal volume.
  • Structure: Aluminum rod spars + aeroply reinforcements; topology optimisation on ribs and plates to reduce mass.

Design summary (point-wise)

  • Airfoil thickened to 120% to increase Cl while trading off some Cd; validated with wind-tunnel Cp vs chord tests.
  • Wing skeleton laser-cut; two 9x0.5 mm aluminum rods and strategic aeroply plates for strength.
  • Topology optimisation reduced rib mass by ~20% and aeroply plates underwent topology refinement for motor and wing mounts.
  • CFD (Spalart-Allmaras) used for wing and fuselage; mesh convergence ensured at 2 mm element size for fuselage and 1.5 mm for wing.
  • FEA static and explicit-dynamic tests on wing mount and fuselage to check stresses and landing survivability.
  • Propulsion: AT2317 T-motor 1400kv, prop 10x8 inch, battery 3S LiPo 850 mAh - endurance 1.8 min (practical bench results match E-calc).

Condensed technical report

Airfoil & wing

  • S1223 selected for high Cl/Cd; thickness increase to 120% chosen after parametric study (see report tables and fig. 2.4).
  • Wing sizing: span 1000 mm, root chord 200 mm, MAC 176.19 mm, taper ratio 0.75.
  • Aileron: span 213 mm, chord 42 mm; aileron moment calculated via XFLR5.
  • FEA of wing: tested to 30 N lift; von Mises max 74.11 MPa and max deflection 12.77 mm for the wing structure model used in ANSYS static analysis (wing mount FOS 3.23).

Fuselage

  • Length 768 mm, maximum equivalent diameter 115 mm, fineness ratio 6.67.
  • Cockpit-style cross-section chosen over circular and square after CFD trade study due to manufacturing, packing, and impact-area considerations (see table of fuselage drag values).
  • Static FEA: fuselage wing mount subjected to 30 N gave max von Mises 4.108 MPa and max deflection 0.0146 mm; explicit-dynamic study confirmed no critical-component failure on belly landing.

Stability & control

  • Static margin computed as 24.08% (Xcg 62.85 mm; Xnp 105.29 mm; MAC 176.19 mm).
  • XFLR5 root-locus and mode analysis show stable longitudinal and lateral modes; one spiral mode slightly right of origin but considered acceptable.

Propulsion & power

  • Thrust requirement 16.72 N derived from W=2.2 kg and T/W target 0.76. Practical thrust bench results gave 15.07 N measured for chosen configuration.
  • Motor: AT2317 1400kv. Prop: 10x8 or 10x7 depending on availability. Battery: LiPo 850 mAh 3S.

Weight & manufacturing

  • Weight build-up table totals 2122 g (payload 1350 g included). Empty weight listed as 772 g.
  • Manufacturing approach: laser-cut aeroply and fliteboard panels, hot-wire for foam, glue & Bondtite joints for critical mounts.
