DDC Micro — 2024

In the 2024 edition, Team Arrow focused on developing a high payload-fraction UAV that balanced lightweight construction with structural strength and aerodynamic efficiency. As the Design Lead, I guided the team to maximize lift and stability while minimizing empty weight.

Design highlights

The aircraft adopted a trailing-edge tapered wing with a 1-meter span, which reduced balsa usage and improved aerodynamic smoothness. A simplified three-walled fuselage constructed from aeroply and flite board delivered an excellent strength-to-weight ratio, enhancing impact resistance and durability during repeated operations. The fuselage and wing geometry were optimized for payload fraction and manufacturing efficiency, allowing rapid repairs and quick turnarounds between flights.

Achievements

  • Overall 2nd Place – DDC Micro Class 2024
  • Best Technical Presentation Award

Optimization & flight performance

Through iterative testing and disciplined refinement, the UAV’s empty weight was reduced from ~1000 g to under 700 g, achieving a payload fraction of 0.72. The simplified fuselage made the aircraft easier to manufacture, repair, and optimize between flights, which nearly doubled the number of successful test sorties. Aerodynamic tuning and structural light-weighting produced measurable gains in stability and mission reliability. A dedicated pilot training program further improved consistency during trials and competition flights.

My role

Design Lead - Led airframe and wing planform design, mass optimization, materials selection, and verification testing.

Key points & lessons learned

  • Trailing-edge taper and optimized span reduced structural weight while preserving lift.
  • Three-walled aeroply + flite board fuselage provided a robust, repairable structure with low empty weight.
  • Process improvements (manufacturing and pilot training) increased successful flight attempts and data collection cadence.

Quick spec (typical)

  • Class: DDC Micro
  • Wing: Trailing-edge tapered, 1 m span
  • Fuselage: Three-walled aeroply + flite board
  • Empty weight: < 700 g (post-optimization)
  • Payload fraction: 0.72
  • Primary materials: Aeroply, flite board, balsa, carbon reinforcements
