DDC Micro — 2023

The 2023 UAV focused on achieving high stability and efficient structural performance, with a strong emphasis on flight control and durability.

Design highlights

The aircraft featured a cranked dihedral wing planform, which substantially improved roll stability and made the UAV easier to handle during turns and maneuvers. A squared semi-monocoque fuselage offered a lightweight yet impact-resistant structure, providing ample internal space for electronics and payload integration. To improve belly-landing resilience, XPS foam was added to the underside to absorb shocks and protect critical components.

Achievements

  • Overall 2nd Place – DDC Micro Class 2023
  • Best Design Report Award

Optimization & flight performance

Throughout the 2023 development phase, the team continuously refined the UAV through experimentation and testing. The wing mounting system evolved from a slotted lock to a more reliable wing-nut assembly, improving pre-flight efficiency and robustness. The replacement of aluminum rods with aeroply shear webs reduced overall weight while maintaining structural integrity. By optimizing aerodynamics and streamlining assembly, the team achieved measurable gains in stability, performance, and operational reliability — improvements that informed the lightweight strategies used in the 2024 design.

My role

Design Engineer — Responsible for airframe layout, structural sizing, material selection, and wing/fuselage interface design. (Highlighted role for portfolio)

Key points & lessons learned

  • Validated cranked dihedral as an effective passive roll-stability mechanism for small UAVs.
  • Semi-monocoque construction delivered high stiffness-to-weight while simplifying payload access.
  • Field testing emphasised the importance of easy-to-change wing attachment for rapid turnarounds.

Quick spec (typical)

  • Class: DDC Micro
  • Airframe: Squared semi-monocoque fuselage
  • Wing: Cranked dihedral planform
  • Landing protection: XPS foam belly panels
  • Primary materials: Aeroply, balsa, carbon reinforcements, foam
