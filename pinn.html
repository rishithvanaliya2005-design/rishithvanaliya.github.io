Physics-Informed Neural Networks (PINNs)

Using Fourier-enhanced PINNs (PhysicsNeMo style) to model 2D incompressible flows — lid-driven cavity and cylinder wake — combining limited simulation data with physical constraints to reconstruct u, v, p fields and extract wake statistics.

Figures & Results Methods & Configs Request Code / Data

Selected highlight: Accepted paper & presentation at FMFP–2025 (Institute of Technology, Nirma University).

Project Summary

This work evaluates architecture choices, optimizer behaviour, and training strategies for PINNs applied to laminar flows. A modified FourierNet (8 layers) delivered consistently better reconstruction fidelity vs standard fully-connected PINNs. Training up to ~8000 steps with Adam/AdamW yielded strong convergence without excessive compute.

Methods & Experimental Setup

  • Equations: 2D incompressible Navier–Stokes (u, v, p) with continuity & momentum residuals enforced in loss.
  • Data: Reference CFD snapshots used as sparse supervision (lid-driven cavity / cylinder wake cases).
  • Model: Fourier-enhanced MLP (Modified FourierNet), 8 hidden layers reported as best tradeoff in experiments.
  • Training: Loss = data + PDE residual + BC loss. Optimizers: Adam & AdamW performed best. Typical training length: up to 8000 steps for convergence.
  • Validation: L2 error vs benchmark (Ghia & Ghia for lid-driven cavity), plus qualitative field comparisons and frequency/wake metrics for cylinder wake.
