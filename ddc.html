Drone Design Challenge (DDC) Micro Class

The SAE Drone Development Challenge (DDC) is a national-level competition that challenges students to design, build, and demonstrate efficient unmanned aerial vehicles (UAVs) capable of carrying payloads within strict dimensional and weight constraints. The Micro Class category emphasizes high payload fractions, compact design, and innovative engineering solutions. The event provides an excellent platform for students to apply aerodynamics, structural design, and systems engineering principles in a practical and competitive environment.

DDC-Micro 2024 – Design Philosophy and Innovation

In the 2024 edition, Team Arrow focused on developing a high payload fraction UAV that balanced lightweight construction with structural strength and aerodynamic efficiency. As the Design Lead, I guided the design process toward achieving maximum lift and stability while minimizing the empty weight of the aircraft. The team adopted a trailing edge tapered wing with a 1-meter span, reducing balsa usage and enhancing aerodynamic smoothness. A three-walled fuselage made of aeroply and flite board provided exceptional strength-to-weight performance, ensuring impact resistance and durability during repeated flight operations.

Achievements

  • Overall 2nd Place – DDC Micro Class 2024
  • Best Technical Presentation Award

Optimization and Flight Performance

Through iterative testing and refinement, the UAV’s empty weight was reduced from 1000 grams to under 700 grams, achieving a remarkable payload fraction of 0.72. The simplified fuselage design made the UAV easier to manufacture, repair, and optimize between flights, nearly doubling the number of successful flight tests. A dedicated pilot training program ensured precision control, enabling consistent performance throughout testing and competition. The 2024 project reflected the team’s evolution toward a more data-driven, performance-oriented development cycle, marked by continuous improvement and engineering discipline.

DDC-Micro 2023 – Stability and Structural Efficiency

The 2023 UAV focused on achieving high stability and efficient structural performance, with a strong emphasis on flight control and durability. The aircraft featured a cranked dihedral wing planform, which substantially improved roll stability and made the UAV easier to handle during turns and maneuvers. A squared semi-monocoque fuselage offered a lightweight yet impact-resistant structure, providing ample internal space for electronics and payload integration. To improve belly landing resilience, XPS foam was added to the underside, effectively absorbing shocks and protecting critical components.

Achievements

  • Overall 2nd Place – DDC Micro Class 2023
  • Best Design Report Award

Optimization and Flight Performance

Throughout the 2023 development phase, the team continuously refined the UAV through experimentation and testing. The wing mounting system evolved from a slotted lock to a more reliable wing nut assembly, improving pre-flight efficiency and robustness. The use of aeroply shear webs in place of aluminum rods reduced overall weight while maintaining structural integrity. By optimizing aerodynamics and streamlining assembly, the team achieved significant gains in stability, performance, and operational reliability. These improvements laid the groundwork for the advanced lightweight strategies and design philosophies implemented in the 2024 UAV.
