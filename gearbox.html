Design of Gearbox

Design Requirements

  • Gear type: Helical
  • Input power: 8 kW
  • Input speed: 750 RPM
  • Required reduction ratio: 8
  • Number of stages: 2-stage reduction

Design Approach

  • Design methodology:
    • Helical gears designed using beam strength theory (Lewis bending equation) as the primary criterion.
    • Design subsequently verified for wear strength to ensure resistance against pitting and surface fatigue.
  • Gear material:
    • Surface hardened, Grade-1 Cast Steel.
    • Surface hardening used to increase wear resistance while maintaining core toughness.
  • Two-stage reduction (overall ratio = 8):
    • Stage-wise PCD values:
      • P₁ = 108.63 mm
      • G₁ = 217.27 mm
      • P₂ = 108.63 mm
      • G₂ = 434.52 mm
    • Module, face width, helix angle selected to satisfy both bending and wear criteria with adequate safety factors.
  • Shaft design:
    • Shafts designed for combined bending and torsion due to transmitted torque and gear forces.
    • Checked for allowable shear stress, deflection limits, and key/hub fitment strength.
